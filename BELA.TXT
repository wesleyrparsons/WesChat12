Many years ago, Bela Julesz, a researcher at Bell Laboratories working in the 
field of perception, used two fields of computer-generated random dots to
explore our ability to see things in 3D.

The BELA program included here will let you see this for yourself.  Run it on
your HP95LX or HP100LX and it will display two random dot fields.  Cross or
relax your eyes so that the vertical line at the left edge merges with the
vertical line between the fields.  Once accomplished, there should be a square
region in the center of the field which seems to be at a different distance
than the rest of the field.

Julesz discovered that certain people with "photographic" memory can view one
field with their left eye, then close that one and subsequently view the other
field with their right eye, and were then able to "see" the stereo vision 
effect using their memory alone!  I first read about this in a Popular 
Psychology article more than 20 years ago.
